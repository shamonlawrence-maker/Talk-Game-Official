The Digital Scribe: Teaching Writing in the Age of AI

Published on August 15, 2024

Artificial intelligence is changing how we think about writing. For many educators, the rise of tools like ChatGPT feels like a threat to traditional writing instruction. But what if we viewed it differently? Instead of fearing it, how can we leverage it as a tool for deeper learning, critical thinking, and creativity?

This post explores some initial ideas and classroom strategies for navigating this new landscape. My goal is not to replace the hard work of writing, but to augment it, using AI as a brainstorming partner, a tireless editor, and a co-creator.

Strategy 1: AI as a "Thought Partner"

Before writing, students can "interview" an AI about their topic. They can ask it to play devil's advocate, to provide counterarguments, or to generate a list of potential research questions. This process can help students clarify their own thinking and approach their topic from multiple angles.

Strategy 2: Deconstructing and Reconstructing

Have students analyze an AI-generated text. What makes it sound generic? Where does it lack a personal voice? Then, challenge them to rewrite it, infusing it with their own style, anecdotes, and analysis. This is a powerful exercise in understanding the nuances of voice and tone.

The journey is just beginning, but I'm optimistic about the possibilities. By teaching students to be the masters of these new tools, not their dependents, we can prepare them for a future where collaboration between human and machine is the norm.
